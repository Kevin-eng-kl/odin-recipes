Recipe of Dosa

Description:

Dosa also known as Dosai, is a classic popular South Indian crepe made with fermented lentil and rice batter. Restaurants often serve them as a meal with coconut chutney and sambar. But they taste equally good with any other spicy chutney or vegetable kurma.

Ingredients:

  • Lentils
  • Rice
  • Cold Filtered Water
  • Salt

Steps:

  1. In a bowl take ½ cup idli rice or parboiled rice along with ½ cup regular rice.
  2. To the same bowl, add ¼ cup urad dal and ⅛ teaspoon fenugreek seeds (methi)
  3. Rinse the rice, lentils and methi seeds together a couple of times and keep them aside.
  4. In a separate bowl, take 2 tablespoons of thick poha
  5. Add 1.5 cups of water and mix. Cover with a lid and soak everything for 4 to 5 hours.
  6. Drain all the water and add the soaked ingredients to a mixer grinder or a blender.
  7. Add ½ teaspoon of edible rock salt. Mix very well. Instead of rock salt, you can use non-iodized salt or sea salt crystals or Himalayan pink salt.
  8. Cover and allow to ferment for 8 to 9 hours or more. The time of fermentation will vary depending on the temperature conditions, climate and altitude.
  9. Heat a cast iron pan. When the pan becomes hot, spread ¼ to ½ teaspoon oil all over the pan with a spoon or with a thick paper towel dipped in oil. For a richer taste, you can cook the dosa with ghee or butter.
  10. Dosa is ready!
